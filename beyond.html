Who I Should BeFRIDAY, JANUARY 18, 2013

Something I've struggled with for years, and still sometimes struggle with, is the concept of who I should be versus who I am. Even though the expectations for people in my demographic are mostly implicit, the pressure of my "should be's" are strong. I should be career driven. I should put off having kids until I have my foot in the door (what door?) and my career defined. I should be part of the rat race and constantly upgrade the "toys" (house, cars, etc.) in my life. I should eat a healthy balanced diet with a little bit of everything. Meat and animal products included. I should put more time into managing how people perceive me and grow my network. I should want more, bigger, and better. Always. But I don't.

I'm done putting energy into things I am supposed to care about. I now want to focus on things that actually matter to me in life. Like my family. My hubby. My health. The simple, organic lifestyle. My happiness and well-being. My community. I want my husband to join me in the freedom lifestyle, to not be over-worked and stressed. But how? How do we carve our path when this society clearly supports a way of life we don't want? We are finding the answers to our questions, slowly but surely. We question everything along the way, and know that the best thing we can do is to trust our hearts. The journey is not clearly defined, and there is no end. But we have committed to letting go of the should be's. And that is a good enough start for us.

My words have been few recently, as much of my journey is inward for now. Lots of healing and growth are taking place. But I wish to share it all with the world one day. To share the light and love that exists.

Have FaithMONDAY, JANUARY 7, 2013

With the start of the new year comes the obligatory reflection of how life has been in the past and new resolutions for the future. However, playing this little exercise with my twin sister started us on the wrong path. Between the two of us, our 20s has been nothing short of completely tumultuous and we recounted tales of failed businesses, cancelled weddings, baby out of wedlock, missed opportunities, numerous job changes (well, this one's just been me), and general misconduct that we choose to constantly blast in a public forum. Like on this blog. And hers.

Anyway. I don't know about resolutions anymore. To me, they are too transactional and task-driven. Run marathon. Check. Buy house. Check. Get married. Check. Instead of a "to do" list this year, what about a "to be" list? A list of attributes and states of mind I want to be in as I navigate yet another year of the roaring 20s? Here are my "to be's" (is this grammatically correct?) for the year:

  1. Have gratitude -- We all know how momentary happiness is. Once we get what we want, that glorious feeling of elation eventually fades. But why let it? Why not be in a constant state of gratitude for the things/people/experiences that we have in life? Why not be grateful for the simple joys in life and focus on the good? I will count my blessing every day, multiple times a day, for just how good I have it. The bad stuff in life just doesn't deserve my energy anymore. I will open my heart and shine light on my life.
  2. Be present -- I've been told, and know to be true, that I am not an active listener. I will look at someone like I am listening to them but my mind has already sped off into another topic completely unrelated. It's not that I don't care about what they are saying...well, I guess my actions sure make it seem that way. This year, I will focus on being present in life and my presence in general. I will savor every moment in this crazy sexy life and not shut people out with my actions. A simple exercise to support this "to be" is to not use my phone when I am talking to others. How rude is texting while having a conversation with someone? But I do it all the time. I love multitasking. But this takes away from my interactions with others and takes away from the experience we are sharing. I think we text while talking to others not just to multitask but also to fill the "uncomfortable" silences. But let's enjoy them instead. So if I text while talking to you, please call me out on it. What I'm texting is not nearly as important as this moment I'm sharing with you.
  3. Have faith -- This "to be" is what originally inspired this post. While texting with my sister (and possibly having a conversation with someone else) about how up and down and down our 20s have been, I spouted out these words of wisdom:Have faith that everything turns out more or less how it's supposed to be. I felt divinely inspired and just had to let her know. In the end, it will all be okay. And knowing this, let's allow ourselves to enjoy the present.

How to Have Your Dream Wedding Without Having to Take Out a Second MortgageMONDAY, NOVEMBER 26, 2012

Today officially marks one month since Lane and I got married. To celebrate my one month wedding anniversary with Lane, I've decided to write a blog post about how I was able to have my dream wedding without breaking the bank or taking out a second mortgage (sad, but true -- I know of people who have taken out loans for their wedding!).

First, here is the number one rule: never, ever, EVER throw a wedding with money you don't have. If you need to take out a loan of any sort, then it's time to think about other options. Remember, as spectacular and amazing as wedding days can be, it ultimately is just ONE day in your life. It's not worth going into debt for. Plus, there are so many ways you can be cost-effective. We chose to have our wedding on a Friday, which cost less than having it on a Saturday or Sunday. We also booked our venue when it was under renovations so we got an additional 15% off. We kept our wedding intimate with just over 70 guests, making costs for food and beverages much more manageable. We saved even more money because we didn't exchange wedding rings -- we don't value symbols and both hate wearing jewelry. Similarly, I didn't buy a garter for a garter toss, had cupcakes instead of a cake (we had Sprinkles cupcakes which our guests LOVED and it cost only half as much as a cake would've), and wore shoes we already own (I know shoes seem like a small detail but brides go crazy spending big $$$ on Jimmy Choos). Another important thing -- get your guests as involved as possible! I wanted everyone to feel engaged at my wedding (have you ever been to a wedding where you just didn't care to be there?) so many people had roles outside of just being a guest. Utilize their talents and resources. I collected glass jars from many family, friends, and neighbors. My sister and godfather were my photographers. And so on.

So remember, just use your creativity and tap into your resources. Let your guests be a part of the experience. Just because something is a tradition doesn't mean you have to have it at your wedding. No need to spend money on silly traditions you don't care about. Also, just because something costs more doesn't mean it's better. And lastly, the most important element to our wedding was our amazing family and friends. With the right people, any event will be spectacular. And friends and family are priceless. :-)

Why Cable Is For DummiesTHURSDAY, SEPTEMBER 20, 2012

Growing up, I worshiped cable. Watching TV was all I wanted to do and all I did when given the chance. My summers consisted of lying on the couch all day watching the summer lineups on my favorite channels, including Disney, Nickelodeon, TLC, and the Food Network. What made this experience even better was mindlessly eating prepackaged food while watching show after show. Sure, I did other things such as karate and swim lessons during the summer time, but those commitments only took up 2-3 hours every day.

I had a friend who didn't have cable and I remember being so shocked when he told me. "So what do you do all day?!", I naively asked.

Ever since I moved out of my parents' house, I vowed to never have cable TV again. The reason why is because the more I opened my eyes, the more I realized how cable is for dummies. It seriously is a brain drain and I wish I had those summers back so I could've done something productive and meaningful with my time. It depresses me to think about all the hours I lost in my life to TV.

  1. Cable costs money. Yes, unless you are mooching off of your neighbor, cable is going to run you $50-200 every month. That's a lot of money to be spending on something you don't need. That's enough money for a week's worth of groceries or to fund a new hobby or two every month. And if there is a show you HAVE to watch, you can probably find it for free online.
  2. Cable makes you a slave. A reason why cable TV is appealing to some is because you can watch an episode when it first premieres. However, then you have to adjust your life to fit around its scheduling. I can't even tell you how many times a phone conversation has been cut short because "My favorite show is on now!" or "I need to go watch my show!". People become slaves to their TV. A great example is my dad, who has a bazillion channels and records shows all the time. He gets stressed out if he can't watch everything because he needs more space to record even more shows. So it becomes this endless cycle of recording, watching, erasing, and recording more. It just never ends. And yes, it's very stressful for him.
  3. Cable makes you passive. And fat. You are sitting in front of a TV for hours on end, probably killing time and eating whatever junk you have in your house. Rather than taking a jog or having dinner with your friend, you are just sitting and watching a box. I can't even recall most of the stuff I've watched, proving how passive we become when watching TV. Very rarely is anything retained.
  4. Cable subjects you to hardcore marketing. 20 minutes out of every hour is spent on commercials. That's one third of your time watching TV that yet another plastic junk or fast food chain is marketed to you. Have you ever seen a commercial where the product or food marketed is something you actually need or is going to enrich your life?
  5. Cable is a clutch. My sister lets the TV run 24/7 because she likes the background noise. Another person I know can only fall asleep with the TV on. Is this normal?

So break free, cut out the noise, and find yourself. Embrace silence. Cherish your time. And enjoy your life. I promise you won't regret it.

How I Lost Money Working My First JobTUESDAY, SEPTEMBER 18, 2012

My twin sister and I have always wanted to work. Something about making our own money was so appealing and our shot at our first job came the the fall of 2000. We were 13 years old and our neighbor came to our house looking for a sub for her son's paper route. He was going out of town for a week and she wondered if we would take his route while he was gone. We were completely in disbelief that our neighbor thought we were responsible enough for such a task. This was our big break. With an immense sense of pride, we agreed to shadow her son for two days to learn the ropes.

And so the week went. We folded, rubber banded, and delivered newspapers to our entire neighborhood everyday after school. We quickly learned the fastest route to take and even enlisted my brother and his friend to help us fold newspapers. This wasn't so bad and we would be paid $60 by the end of the week! The only day we were dreading was our last day of work, which was a Sunday. Sundays played by a different set of rules and all newspapers had to be delivered by 7am, which meant we needed to wake up at 4:30am to start folding. I remember my sister and I getting out of bed and setting up shop in the garage with wind howling outside and us shivering in the cold. Somehow, we made it through the darkness on our bikes and had every house delivered except for one last row of houses. We sighed with relief and decided that the quickest way to complete this last set was to throw the newspaper on the porch rather than walking it up. This was a great plan and by our last house, I had gotten really confident with my throwing. I took a deep breathe and threw the newspaper on the porch. Except it didn't hit the porch. I had thrown it at a window and the window cracked.

We heard the people inside say, "What was that?!", and of course the logical thing to do was to hit our bikes and get the heck out of there. When we got home, we confessed to our parents what had happened and they insisted we go back and let the people know. Long story short, the window cost $100 to fix so the $60 we made went straight into the window, along with an additional $40.

We were down for a while about this whole experience but rather than letting it keep us down, we moved on. Our neighbor's son eventually offered us his route permanently, and along with our younger brother, we built a monopoly of newspaper routes in our town. Doing the paper route every day gave us exposure to many of our neighbors who offered us babysitting jobs on the side. By the time I was 16, I had saved $4,500 from my jobs, all stemmed from that ill-fated first week.

The moral of this story is that breaking windows and falling down will happen. Just don't let it discourage you from getting back up and taking another swing at it.

Here I am, a 25-year-old soon to be married corporate professional completely buckling and becoming weak at the knees for a boy band.

I spent the next few days on a complete One Direction high. I Googled and Youtubed everything I could about them, watching live performances and wondering which boy I was most astrologically compatible with (Harry, I'm looking at you). Something about these guys made me feel alive and I realized I was envious of their vitality, their hunger and drive chasing relentlessly after their dreams. Somewhere in the last few post-college years, I had lost that anything-is-possible mentality and settled upon an expected life, working a job I don't love and searching for God knows what. I lost the courage to fight for the things I believe in and instead settled for whatever came the easiest. I could justify up and down that I am successful and happy, but One Direction made me realize I had to make some changes and not give up on me. I, too, can be sparkling happy and full of life.
